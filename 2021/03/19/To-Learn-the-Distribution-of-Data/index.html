To Learn the Distribution of the Data

Posted by Changtao Li on 2021-03-19

With or Without Missing Data

People generally categorize machine learning as pairs like : supervised learning and unsupervised learning, classification or regression, etc. However, the boundary of these categories are sometimes blurry. For example, we often encounter words like semi-supervised learning. From time to time, we are confused by these categories. But we can circumvent this blurry area by focusing at the data with which we are dealing.

There are two kinds of data: with or without missing data. The meaning of ‘missing’ is not so obvious. Take MNIST dataset for example. If you want to classify different digits, then the MNIST dataset is enough. You have different pictures of different digits and each is assigned with a label. So, you can use methods like logistic regression, random forest or neural network to achieve you goal. But if your goal is to generate some digit pictures, then MNIST dataset is a dataset with missing data. Here is the reason. To generate something, we need the distribution of the data. Mixture of many Gaussian Distribution can approximate any distribution. So, we can use GMM(Gaussian Mixture Model) to learn the distribution of the data. But to learn the parameters of this model, we need know which pictures are generated by the same distribution. In general, by no means can we know this. Therefore, it’s called latent variable and MNIST becomes a dataset with missing data here.

After clarifying the meaning of missing data, the next question is how to fit a dataset with missing data.

Typically we don’t know the latent variable, but its information is contained in the posterior. SO we can consider its expectation under the posterior distribution of the latent variables(E-Step), then we maximize this expectation to find a new estimate for the parameters(M-Step). This is the idea of EM algorithm.
