Generate the Specific Data You Want

Posted by Changtao Li on 2021-03-25

Background

GAN can learn the distribution of a dataset and sample from the distribution. For example, if you train a GAN on pictures of handwritten digits(0, 1, …, 9), then your generator will produce handwritten digits. That is, given a latent vector, the generator will yield a picture of a handwritten digits, but you will never know what digit is on that picture unless you spend a very long time figuring out the function of each weight of the generator. So, here is the problem: how to generate only 8 using this generator?

Multi-task Learning

Some clever people will say, you can train a GAN just on pictures of 8. Then, you generator is ignorant about the distribution of other numbers. As a result, it cannot produce other pictures but 8!

Brilliant idea! But if you reconsider this method, you’ll find it Penny wise and pound-foolish! Data is expensive, and you desert 90% of this dataset just to create a generator only capable of producing 8.

A more reasonable explanation is this: pictures of different digits share a lot in common, like the tiny structures. If you desert these data, then it’s unlikely for you to learn the common representation of digit pictures thus impairing the quality of your pictures. Actually, this is called multi-learning. You can search for Hierarchy Bayes Model to learn more about it.
